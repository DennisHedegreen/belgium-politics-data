# Belgium Website-Fit Boundary

Internal scope guard.

Belgium must not become a broad Belgian politics portal.

The website-fit version is a narrow source-proof tool:

- one federal election family first: Chamber of Representatives
- one election year first: `2024`
- one readable geography layer first: municipality-level rows after the Belgium
  geography audit
- a small number of boring structural factors only after coded/current source
  coverage is proven
- no public app/profile exposure until the page can be explained in one short
  TID-style tool description

## What This Protects

Hedegreen Research is not trying to host a full country encyclopedia, a polling
dashboard, or a live political monitoring product.

The politics-data tools should stay small enough to fit the website:

- a clear question
- a clear dataset
- a visible method boundary
- a public-safe result surface
- no pretend comprehensiveness

For Belgium, the useful first claim is not "Belgian politics data is done."

The useful first claim is:

> Here is one audited Belgian federal election layer, joined only to factors
> whose geography and source coverage can be defended.

## Explicit Non-Scope

Do not add these to Belgium until the narrow Chamber tool is already stable and
there is a separate public-surface decision:

- municipal council elections
- regional parliament elections
- European Parliament elections
- historical Chamber series
- candidate-level analysis
- coalition simulation
- polling or live news
- language-community party-family aggregation
- broad policy dashboards
- every plausible Statbel factor
- cross-country ranking claims

## First Public Shape

If Belgium ever reaches public preview, the first website-fit shape should be:

- title: `Belgian Politics Data`
- status: public preview or internal preview, not final
- election: `2024 Chamber`
- geography: bridge-safe municipality layer only
- factors: only the minimal defended set, likely `population`,
  `population_density`, and `age65_pct`
- wording: non-causal pattern reader, not explanation engine

## Promotion Rule

Belgium may expand only one layer at a time.

Expansion order:

1. prove source and geography
2. add one factor family
3. add coverage checks
4. add app wording
5. then consider another factor or year

If a proposed feature makes the public page harder to explain, it waits.
